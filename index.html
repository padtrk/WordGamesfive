    How a reading is scored

    Your line is run through the machine and its odds on the next word are set beside the hidden line's. The score is the share of the machine's ten favourite bets that your line reproduces, word by word, each capped at what the hidden line itself staked. Reproduce the whole book and score 100; send the machine somewhere else entirely and score nothing.

    Why the original is not required

    Countless different lines drive the machine to the same bets — a graveyard, a haunted house and a séance all summon ghost. The machine's odds are a function of meaning in bulk, not of exact words, so the game scores the destination, not the route. Finding any line that lands there is the whole skill.

    The live sheet

    Unlike its companion games, this one cannot be prepared in advance: your line does not exist until you write it, so every reading is a fresh forward pass of the model, on your device, at the moment you press the button. The hidden lines' bets are set once at warm-up; yours are set live, every round.

    The honest caveat

    A 360-million-parameter model, tuned to sound like an assistant, sets these odds — so its bets are rough and a little assistant-flavoured. A high score means your line and the hidden one read alike to this machine; the great models might disagree. Nothing typed here leaves the browser.
